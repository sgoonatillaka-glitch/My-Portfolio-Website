One step to Novelist

Ever since preschool, I loved storybooks. I didn’t just read them—I saw them. I imagined worlds beyond reality, filled with colours, rain, wild plants, and quiet magic. I played pretend a lot, building houses from cardboard and parks from fallen branches and leaves. Everywhere I looked, there was a story waiting to be told.

I began as a storyteller long before I used pen and paper. Eventually, I wanted my stories to live on the page. I wrote and drew together, inspired by the authors and illustrators I admired. I wrote short pieces, but never a big project—a novel, something longer to dwell in and cherish. Writing was my passion, but it was never seen as a career, not by others and slowly, not even by me. I lost interest, found it again during the pandemic, and lost it once more when life demanded attention elsewhere.

Still, writing is a journey—and journeys can be restarted. I’m a beginner with no fixed method or expectations. For a long time, I wrote only for myself, storing my stories quietly away. Sharing them felt difficult, especially writing in a foreign language that somehow came more easily than my own. But I’m willing to learn, to improve, and to try.

At heart, I’ve always been a storyteller. I’m drawn to fantasy and romance, with sci-fi as a side-kick, often blending genres freely. This space is where I share that journey—my first novel, a work in progress, and a dream I’m finally choosing not to abandon.

This is me, starting again
